The policy of having two Senators per state regardless of population gives smaller states a larger proportion of power, which was part of the "Great Compromise" with the House of Representatives (where larger states have more proportionate power). Distortion is a measure of how much giving each state two senators benefits the state as compared to giving each state power proportional to its population.
We define a state's distortion to measure the extent to which the two senator policy benefits that state. The distortion of a state is defined to be 2 minus the number of senators that the state would have if the same total number of U.S. senators were allocated in proportion to state population. That is to say, the distortion of a given state is
2 - (total # of senators) * (pop. of state) / (total pop.)
States with positive distortion have more senators and therefore more power than would be allocated by population, while states with negative distortion have fewer.
